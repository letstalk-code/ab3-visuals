Virtual staging is all about the software, but there is more to it than that. Traditional home staging costs more money than virtual home staging. It gives real estate agents the tools they need to virtually stage a home. When you stage a house, you make it look more appealing to potential buyers by using different furniture, hanging art on the walls, and putting flowers on the coffee table.

With Virtual Staging, you can do all of these things online. You don't pay for furniture, moving furniture, cleaning the place, putting in flowers, or doing anything else to get the house ready to sell.

Conventional home staging does make money, but for some sellers, the cost upfront may not be worth it. For example, a mid-sized or smaller house might not need full staging, which could cost thousands of dollars.

Virtual staging designers can find the perfect pieces to add to a design because there are so many different styles, colors, and sizes to choose from. Images can always show how houses are set up with the most up-to-date furniture and accessories — making virtual staging an even better option for houses with a unique look, style, floor plan, or color scheme.

## How Virtual Staging Actually Sells Houses

The best virtual staging doesn't work because it has pretty edited photos. Virtual staging only works if it brings in more buyers and gets more money for the house.

When buyers look at a house and start to picture themselves living there, that's when things really start to move. It's much easier for them to picture having parties or raising kids in a home with furniture than in one with empty rooms.

Combining virtual staging with a good marketing plan can make it even more effective. Here are a few simple ways to use virtual staging to sell your homes:

- Post on social media, including Facebook Marketplace
- Create a free video slideshow
- Email your contact list
- Run a Facebook ad campaign
- Print brochures
